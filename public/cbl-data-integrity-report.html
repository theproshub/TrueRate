1. Executive Summary

During routine data validation, TrueRate's automated quality-assurance system identified an inconsistency in the Monetary Policy Rate (MPR) series published through the CBL DataWarehousePro API.

The April 2025 MPR observation is recorded as 17.30 percent. Based on our analysis of the full rate-change history, supporting MPC communiqués, and standard central bank rate-setting conventions, the correct value is 17.25 percent.

This 5-basis-point discrepancy has cascaded forward through all subsequent observations. The current rate is displayed as 16.30 percent in the DataWarehousePro, while the official MPC-determined rate is 16.25 percent.

Key Finding The April 2025 observation for LBR_INR_MPR_1 appears to contain a data-entry error: 17.30 was recorded instead of 17.25. This error propagates to all subsequent periods, causing the current rate to display as 16.30 instead of the MPC-set rate of 16.25.

2. How the Error Was Detected

2.1 Basis-Point Increment Analysis

Central banks worldwide — including the CBL — adjust policy rates in standardized increments, most commonly multiples of 25 basis points (0.25 percentage points). We extracted the complete MPR rate-change history from the DataWarehousePro (87 monthly observations, March 2019 through May 2026) and tested whether each rate change conforms to this convention.

Result: Nine out of ten rate changes are exact multiples of 25 basis points. The sole exception is April 2025, where a +30 basis point move was recorded. A 30bp adjustment has no precedent in the CBL's rate-setting history, nor is it standard practice at any central bank globally. This strongly indicates a data-entry error rather than an intentional policy decision.

2.2 Cascade Analysis

The October 2025 rate cut of 100 basis points was correctly applied in relative terms. However, because it was subtracted from the incorrect April 2025 base of 17.30, it produced an incorrect result:

This means every observation from April 2025 onward is overstated by 5 basis points.

2.3 Cross-Reference with MPC Communiqué

The Q2 2026 MPC communiqué (April 27, 2026) confirmed the policy rate was held at 16.25 percent, consistent with our corrected value and inconsistent with the 16.30 percent stored in the DataWarehousePro.

4. Downstream Impact

The DataWarehousePro API is consumed by multiple data platforms and publications. Incorrect MPR values can affect:

  • Third-party financial data providers that syndicate CBL data, potentially republishing the incorrect 16.30% figure
  • Interest rate spread calculations — the spread between the policy rate and commercial lending rates is misstated by 5bp
  • Monetary policy analysis — the cumulative easing since September 2023 appears as 3.70 percentage points instead of the actual 3.75 percentage points
  • Public trust — discrepancies between published data and MPC communiqués can undermine confidence in official statistics

5. Recommended Correction

Proposed Fix Update the 14 affected rows in the DataWarehousePro database to reflect the MPC-determined rates of 17.25% (Apr–Sep 2025) and 16.25% (Oct 2025–present).

The following SQL reflects the required corrections (adapt table and column names to match the DataWarehousePro schema):

-- Correct Apr 2025 through Sep 2025: 17.30 → 17.25
UPDATE observations
SET    value = 17.25
WHERE  mnemonic = 'LBR_INR_MPR_1'
  AND  period_date >= '2025-04-01'
  AND  period_date <  '2025-10-01'
  AND  value = 17.30;

-- Correct Oct 2025 onward: 16.30 → 16.25
UPDATE observations
SET    value = 16.25
WHERE  mnemonic = 'LBR_INR_MPR_1'
  AND  period_date >= '2025-10-01'
  AND  value = 16.30;

After applying the correction, we recommend verifying the full series by confirming that every rate change is a multiple of 25 basis points.

6. Prevention Recommendation

To prevent similar errors in the future, we respectfully suggest the following:

  1. Validation rule on entry: Flag any MPR change that is not a multiple of 25 basis points for manual review before it is committed to the database.
  2. Cross-check against communiqué: After each MPC meeting, verify that the rate entered into the DataWarehousePro matches the figure in the published communiqué.
  3. Downstream notification: Consider notifying registered API consumers when corrections are made to published series, so they can update their own systems.
